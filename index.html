🍽 What We'll Eat Along the Way

A culinary journey through 4 cities · June 16 – July 2, 2026

🇬🇧 London

Borough Market

Dozens of stalls with fresh cheeses, meats, seafood, and produce. One of the world's great food markets.

Must Try
Afternoon Tea

Scones, finger sandwiches, cakes, pastries with tea or coffee. The quintessential British experience.

British Pub Food

Fish & chips, meat pies, bangers & mash — paired with a local ale or bitter at a cozy pub.

🇫🇷 Paris

Must Try
French Pastries

Buttery croissants, éclairs, macarons, mille-feuille. Ladurée for macarons, Angelina's for hot chocolate.

Crêpes

Sweet and savory — street vendors and sit-down crêperies everywhere. Try Carette or Breizh Café.

Parisian Picnic

Fresh baguettes, cheese, charcuterie, and wine from Rue Cler market — on the lawn at Champ de Mars.

🇮🇹 Rome

Roman Pizza

Wood-fired, thin and crispy. Try pizza al taglio (by the slice) from a street counter.

Must Try
The Four Roman Pastas

Cacio e Pepe, Carbonara, Amatriciana, Gricia — all built on Pecorino Romano, guanciale, and black pepper.

Gelato

Denser and silkier than ice cream. Best test of a gelateria: the pistachio. Look for natural colors.

🇪🇸 Barcelona

Must Try
Tapas

Patatas bravas, croquetas, bombas, boquerones. Eaten standing at a bar counter with cold beer or vermouth.

Fresh Seafood

Arroz negro (black rice with squid ink), grilled dorada, and fresh boquerones straight from the Boqueria.

Paella

Traditional Spanish rice dish with saffron, vegetables, and protein — chicken, rabbit, or seafood. Order for the table.

🌊 Beaches & Day Trips

Coastal adventures & crystal waters · June 24–30, 2026

Santa Marinella
📍 Near Rome · June 25 · 45–60 min by train

"Pearl of the Tyrrhenian Sea." Sandy shores, turquoise water, mix of free public beach and beach clubs. Great for swimming and sunbathing in the sun.

Tossa de Mar
📍 Costa Brava · June 29 · ~1.5 hrs from BCN

Stunning clear-water beaches framed by a 14th-century medieval castle (Vila Vella). Small sheltered coves with some of the clearest water on the Costa Brava.

Barceloneta
📍 Barcelona · June 30 · 20 min walk from centre

Historic urban beach in the old fishing district. Lively atmosphere, water sports, beach bars (chiringuitos), and excellent seafood. Fun fact: the sand was imported from Egypt in 1992.

✨ First Stop

London

Cheers, mate! 🎉

🇬🇧 United Kingdom June 16–17 1.5 Days Arrive: Heathrow (LHR) Nana: 6:50 AM · Bebe + Fam: 10:15 AM

Fun Facts: London

🕐
Big Ben's Lean

Due to tunnel excavations, the Elizabeth Tower leans 1.5 feet northwest. Its time is kept accurate using a stack of old English penny coins on the pendulum.

👑
The Flag Code

You can tell if the King is home at Buckingham Palace by the flagpole. Royal Standard = King in residence. Union Jack = King is away.

📚
Poets' Corner

Westminster Abbey's south transept commemorates over 100 writers including Shakespeare, Jane Austen, and Charles Dickens — a hallowed literary space for centuries.

✈️
Secret Tunnels

Legend has it Buckingham Palace has a network of tunnels beneath it connecting to nearby streets, built originally for heating and mail.

Day 1 — Tuesday, June 16 Afternoon Tea Bus · Big Ben · Westminster
🏛 Culture
2:30 PM – 4:00 PM
Afternoon Tea Bus Tour
Double-decker tour with afternoon tea — scones, finger sandwiches, and cakes served while rolling past Big Ben, Houses of Parliament, and Westminster Abbey.
🏛 Culture
Westminster Abbey
Gothic church at the heart of British history since 1066. Don't miss Poets' Corner — Shakespeare, Austen, and Dickens are all commemorated here. 16 royal weddings held here, most recently William and Catherine in 2011.
🏛 Culture
Big Ben & Buckingham Palace
"Big Ben" is actually the 13-ton bell inside the Elizabeth Tower — kept accurate by old English pennies on the pendulum. At Buckingham Palace: if the Royal Standard is flying, the King is home. Watch for the Changing of the Guard.
🌿 Outdoor
London Eye
Panoramic views of the entire city from the 135-metre observation wheel on the South Bank of the Thames.
Day 2 — Wednesday, June 17 British Museum · Borough Market · Covent Garden
🏛 Culture
10:00 AM – 12:00 PM
British Museum
World-class collection of 8 million objects. Rosetta Stone, Elgin Marbles, Egyptian mummies, and the Lewis Chessmen all under one spectacular roof. Free entry.
🍽 Food

London's most famous food market — dozens of stalls with fresh cheeses, meats, seafood, and street food. Try the pork belly sandwich or fresh oysters. A foodie highlight of the trip.
🌿 Outdoor
Covent Garden
Shopping, street performers, boutiques, and one of London's most charming pedestrian squares. A perfect final afternoon before tomorrow's early Eurostar.

Paris

Bonjour, Eiffel Tower! 🗼

🇫🇷 France June 18–21 4 Days Arrive: Gare du Nord 10:59 AM Depart: Jun 22, CDG 11:40 AM

Fun Facts: Paris

🗼
The Tower's Seasonal Lean

The Eiffel Tower tilts away from the sun by up to 7 inches (18 cm) because sunlight only heats one side at a time, expanding that side more than the others.

✈️
Arc de Triomphe Flythrough

Three weeks after WWI's victory parade in 1919, a pilot flew his biplane right through the center of the arch. 660 generals' names are engraved on the inner walls.

🎠
Chantilly's Whipped Cream

The estate invented Crème Chantilly. The Prince of Condé also believed he'd be reincarnated as a horse — so he built his stables as a palace for a royal stallion.

🔥
The Eternal Flame

The flame beneath the Arc de Triomphe has burned continuously since 1923 — rekindled every evening at 6:30 PM without missing a single night, even through WWII occupation.

Roma

Che Bello! 🏛

🇮🇹 Italy June 22–26 5 Days Stay: Airbnb, Aurelio area Arrive: FCO 1:45 PM

Fun Facts: Rome

🐱
Cats are Citizens

Rome's cats are legally protected "bio-patrimony." At Largo di Torre Argentina — where Julius Caesar was assassinated — there is a famous protected cat sanctuary.

⛲
Trevi's Daily Fortune

~€3,000 is tossed into the Trevi Fountain daily — over $1M/year. The city collects it every morning and donates it to Caritas, which provides food for those in need.

🏟
The Lost Marble

The Colosseum was originally covered in sparkling white marble with bronze statues in the arches. Most was stripped away in the Middle Ages to build St. Peter's Basilica.

🕳
The Pantheon's Open Roof

The 27-foot oculus is the building's only light source. When it rains inside the Pantheon, the floor's hidden drains whisk the water away.

🚰
2,000 Fountains

Rome has more fountains than any city on Earth. The small iron nasoni (big noses) on street corners run cold, clean, safe-to-drink water. A lifesaver on a June afternoon!

✨ Last Stop

Barcelona

¡Vamos a España! 🎉

🇪🇸 Spain June 27–30 4 Days Stay: Eixample neighbourhood Arrive: BCN 12:25 PM

Fun Facts: Barcelona

🏖
Egyptian Beaches

Barcelona had no beaches until 1992! Before the Olympics, the coastline was industrial. The city imported tons of sand — some from Egypt — to create 4.5km of beach.

🗽
A Statue of Liberty

Barcelona has its own original Statue of Liberty — one of only three in the world — located inside the beautiful Arús Library.

⛪
God's Height Limit

Gaudí believed no man-made structure should surpass God's creation, so the central Sagrada Família tower is designed to be exactly 1 meter shorter than Montjuïc Hill.

🪑
The Ergonomic Bench

Gaudí designed Parc Güell's mosaic bench by having a worker sit in wet plaster to get the perfect human back shape — then built the curves around the impression.

⏳
Slower than the Pyramids

Construction on Sagrada Família began in 1882 — it has officially taken longer to build than the Great Pyramids of Giza, which only took about 20 years.

Day 1 — Saturday, June 27 Arrival · Neighbourhood Exploring
🌿 Outdoor
Afternoon (2–3 hrs)
Neighbourhood Hopping Walking Tour
Three contrasting areas: Eixample (Modernist grid, wide boulevards), Gothic Quarter (medieval labyrinth with Roman ruins underfoot), and Las Ramblas (Barcelona's famous tree-lined promenade).
Day 2 — Sunday, June 28 Sagrada Família · Hop-On Hop-Off · Parc Güell
🏛 Culture
10:30 AM
Sagrada Família + Passion Tower
Gaudí hated straight lines — the pillars inside look like a stone forest branching toward the ceiling. Construction began in 1882, longer than the Great Pyramids took. Book tower access well in advance.
🍽 Food
Lunch: Los Bellota or Casa Angela
Both are excellent spots near Sagrada Família for traditional Catalan and Spanish dishes.
🏛 Culture
Hop-On Hop-Off Tour or Parc Güell
Gaudí's Parc Güell — originally a failed luxury housing project. The famous mosaic bench was ergonomically shaped by Gaudí having a worker sit in wet plaster to perfect the curves.
Day 3 — Monday, June 29 Tossa de Mar · Sunset Sail
🌊 Beach
Morning
Tossa de Mar — Costa Brava
Crystal-clear water framed by a 14th-century medieval castle (Vila Vella). Small sheltered coves, some of the clearest water on the Costa Brava.
🌿 Outdoor
Alternative
Montserrat Mountain Monastery ⛰
Dramatic mountain monastery 1 hour from Barcelona. Cable car rides, wine tasting, and spectacular ridge views.
🌿 Outdoor
Evening
Sunset Sail Around Barcelona ⛵
Two-hour sail along the Barcelona coastline with cava and snacks included. Watch the sun set behind Montjuïc while the city lights come on.
Day 4 — Tuesday, June 30 Parc Güell · Beach Day · Boqueria · El Nacional
🌿 Outdoor
Morning
Parc Güell — Gaudí's Colorful Mosaic Park
The fairy-tale entrance pavilions (inspired by Hansel & Gretel) are covered in trencadís mosaic. The main terrace view over Barcelona is excellent.
🌊 Beach
Beach Day
Choose your vibe: Bogatell (quieter, local), Nova Icaria (family-friendly), or Barceloneta (lively, historic fishing district). All have clean, warm water.
🍽 Food
Lunch: La Boqueria Market
Barcelona's iconic covered market. Fresh fruit, jamón, Iberian cheese, seafood stools. Arrive before 10am to beat crowds.
🍽 Food
Final Dinner: El Nacional
Grand multi-concept food hall in a restored garage near Passeig de Gràcia. Jamón ibérico, Spanish tortilla, fresh seafood tapas. The perfect farewell meal.
